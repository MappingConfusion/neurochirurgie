Dr Stone qualified as a medical doctor in 1992 at Stellenbosch University and as a neurosurgeon in 2001 at Groote Schuur Hospital. He completed two neurosurgery training fellowships in the United Kingdom (2003–2006) before returning to South Africa to establish his practice.

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers about neurosurgery and our practice.

What conditions does a neurosurgeon treat?
Neurosurgeons treat conditions affecting the brain, spine and peripheral nerves. This includes brain tumours, slipped discs, spinal stenosis, pinched nerves, chronic pain, movement disorders, nerve injuries and head or spine trauma.
What is minimally invasive spine surgery?
Minimally invasive spine surgery uses smaller incisions and specialised instruments to reduce muscle damage, blood loss and pain, often allowing a faster recovery than traditional open surgery.
What is Deep Brain Stimulation (DBS)?
DBS is a surgical treatment where a small device sends electrical impulses to specific areas of the brain. It can help reduce symptoms of Parkinson’s disease, essential tremor and other movement disorders in carefully selected patients.
